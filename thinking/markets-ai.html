AI & Market Shifts

05 may 2026 · 3 exchanges

you
How might widespread AI adoption reshape the competitive dynamics in traditional markets? I'm thinking about moats, commoditization, and who wins in the next phase.
Claude
The immediate effect is commoditization of knowledge work and optimization tasks. But the real shift is more subtle: AI doesn't just compress margins—it changes who can participate at all. You need capital for compute, data ownership becomes more valuable than ever, and speed of iteration matters more than previous advantage. Legacy moats get destroyed faster, but new ones form around data and optimization capabilities.
you
So the winners aren't just the companies with AI capabilities, but the ones who can best leverage their existing data assets and move faster?
Claude
Exactly. But there's a timing component. In phase 1 (now), it's about applying AI to existing operations—margins compress. Phase 2 is about new business models that weren't possible before. The companies that win long-term are those who figure out what becomes possible when computation is cheap and fast. Most are still in phase 1, optimizing the old model.
you
That makes sense. So what should investors be looking for in phase 2 companies? What signals would show someone is thinking about new models, not just optimizing?
Claude
Look for: (1) building products that were technically infeasible before, (2) acquiring customers in new categories that didn't exist, (3) willing to cannibalize existing revenue models, (4) organized around speed of iteration rather than operational efficiency, (5) data accumulation that creates compounding moats. Phase 2 winners often look weird in phase 1 because they're not optimizing for current constraints.
